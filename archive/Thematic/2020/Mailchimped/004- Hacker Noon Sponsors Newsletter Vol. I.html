This Month's Focus: Customized Campaigns

Partners of Hacker Noon - July Edition 


Welcome to our first-ever Partners of Hacker Noon Newsletter, where we spotlight Good Companies and share tips to help you, our partners, achieve your unique marketing goals. 
 

Hacker Noon's two biggest sponsors during the first half of 2020 were Heroku and Radix. 

While Heroku's primary audience is developers who are building cloud-friendly applications, Radix's primary audience is entrepreneurs looking to carve a place for themselves on the Internet.

This is a breakdown of how we helped them achieve their marketing goals using Hacker Noon sponsorship collaterals alone
 

Everybody Asks - WIIFM? (What’s in it for me?)

 


Instead of yes-manning the customers in the interest of shortening the negotiation cycle, we took the time to understand their WIIFM to create poignant campaigns. 

Heroku wanted to increase the reach of its podcast — code[ish] with programmers.

Radix wanted to make .tech domains a viable choice for technologists looking to build websites. 

This meant that there'd be none of that one-shoe-fits-all campaign designing since the target audience was very distinct.

 

The Dreaded Campaign Designing Conundrum

 


We tried to look into the similarities of both companies' requirements first. 

Both Heroku and Radix needed more “visibility”, which made our Top nav Billboard program a perfect starting point.  

This sponsorship package helps both companies reach as many people as possible, widening their brand appeal and exposure.

  • 200k Pageviews daily

  • 700-1000 clicks daily

  • 33% traffic from N. America, Europe, and Asia each


We custom-designed thematic newsletters to target segments of our audience. (Big Shoutout to our Software Development Editor Arthur Tkachenko who’s been championing this area of the business!)

Combining billboard advertisements and thematic newsletters proved to be the right choice while designing and also eventually as it allowed:
  • Readers to skim content without having to click
  • Heroku and Radix to monitor ad-copies' performance 

That was our win-win strategy.
 

Strengthening By Stilting The Roots


Without making it a biology lesson, here’s a self-explanatory picture:

 


Similar to stilt roots that prop up plants that grow on unstable grounds (near ponds, rivers etc), advertising campaigns need supporting structures to help buttress the customers’ requirements.

To this effect, Hacker Noon offered Brand As Author stories, Social Media Support, and Noonifications to buttress the campaign while ensuring:
 
  • Cost-Effective Continuous Limelight
  • Thought Leadership Positioning via Blogs
  • Leverage Hacker Noon's 100,000+ SM followers/connections


Interestingly, when asked to choose between more noonifications and more blog posts, the companies held an opposing view.

Heroku decided to create and publish original content for Hacker Noon readers while Radix decided to leverage Noonifications to also A/B test their marketing copies with each noonification.

At the end of the campaign, Heroku's stories on Hacker Noon amassed a total reading time of 5 days; and Radix's AD via noonifications achieved over 2000 clicks.


Gotta Have Them Freebies

 

Going above and beyond is the Hacker Noon way and that was what we did. 

Both Radix and Heroku were provided with additional ad slots to such an extent that Heroku alone reaped free airtime to the tune of almost 40% of the total deal size.

No customer has ever complained about over-delivery and we can go out on a limb and say that none are about to start doing that..ever.
