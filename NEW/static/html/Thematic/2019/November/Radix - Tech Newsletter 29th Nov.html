How To Name Your Next Unicorn

 

 


How did I pick a name for my first company? I and my partner were working in my kitchen. We borrowed a second laptop from my girlfriend to code at night. Sometime past midnight, she passed me this box of awesome nuts from Syria. On the back, I saw a nutritional label with the Net Weight value written in a cool font. It clicked for us and we named our company just that — netWeight.

What next? My next project was related to creating grocery lists and I simply used a name generator and picked — Groceristar.
 

Next. I'm trying to promote my country - Ukraine, in some way. So for my future food-tech holding, I needed something delicious and relatable to the US users. So I named it — ChickenKyiv.

Disclaimer: We have a restaurant in Kyiv with the same name. I’m happy to compete with its owner, Dmytro Borisov —  a great businessman and patriot.
 

I think it is evident that I don’t really give much thought to naming my companies and projects. Which is fine by me. But, there’s a valuable lesson to be learned from companies that do that. Entire marketing budgets are allocated to rebrandings and renamings because they have shown to deliver tangible and intangible returns much bigger than the investment into renaming.
 

In today’s time, it’s also about domain names. We all know the story behind facebook.com vs thefacebook.com. Domain names coupled with complementary domains such as .tech or a .email to suggest their areas of business. For example, you’re more likely to click on extreme.tech than on extremetech.co.au. Extreme.tech is easier to remember as well.
