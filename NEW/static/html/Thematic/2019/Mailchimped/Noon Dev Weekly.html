Hey Hackers

I recently read this quote from Kurt Vonnegut's Hocus Pocus that goes: "Another flaw in the human character is that everybody wants to build and nobody wants to do maintenance."
This is especially true for developers, who often think the best way to improve a project is by flipping the table and starting from scratch. I, myself, am guilty of having done this, but as Joel Spolsky pointed out ages ago, throwing the baby out with the bathwater isn't always the best approach. I tip my hat to Slack here, who significantly improved their desktop app's performance recently with a bit by bit approach to refactoring.
